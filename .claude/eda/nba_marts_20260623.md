## Suggested dbt Models

> These are already-built marts, so there's no staging to scaffold. Instead: data-quality hardening and net-new models the profile supports.

### Hardening (existing models)
- **Fix shooting percentages** in `nba_player_career_stats` and `nba_player_season_stats` — recompute as `SUM(made)/NULLIF(SUM(attempted),0)` so values stay in `[0,1]`, then add `dbt_utils.accepted_range(min_value=0, max_value=1)` tests on every `*_pct`.
- **Add `unique` + `not_null` tests** on the grain keys: `nba_team_dim.team_id`, `nba_player_career_stats.person_id`, `nba_player_advanced_career.person_id`, and the composite keys of the season marts.
- **Null-code sentinels**: `draft_year` negatives → NULL; document `current_era_active_till = 2100` as "ongoing".
- **`TRIM(current_team_abbrev)`** in `nba_team_dim`.
- **Normalise `game_type`** cup labels (`NBA Cup` / `Emirates NBA Cup` / `NBA Emirates Cup` / `in-season-knockout` → one canonical value), ideally in staging so all marts inherit it.
- **Add a minutes/games floor** to `nba_player_advanced_career` (or a `qualified` boolean) so cameo outliers (TS% 1.5, ratings 0/300) don't distort leaderboards.

### Potential new marts
- `nba_team_season_combined` — join `nba_team_season_summary` ⋈ `nba_team_advanced_profile` on `(team_id, season_start_year)`. **Grain:** team-season. **Question:** "Did a team's win record match the efficiency (net rating, pace, shot profile) behind it?" The two marts are explicitly flagged as `correlates_with` each other and share a key — this is the obvious next mart.
- `nba_player_season_enriched` — join `nba_player_season_stats` ⋈ `nba_team_dim` on `primary_team_id`. **Grain:** player-season-gametype. **Question:** "Season production with readable team labels and regular-season vs playoff splits" — directly dashboard-ready.
- `nba_rivalries` — `nba_team_head_to_head` ⋈ `nba_team_dim` (twice, on team and opponent). **Grain:** ordered team pair. **Question:** "Most lopsided all-time rivalries by readable franchise name." Collapsing the mirrored pairs also yields a one-row-per-rivalry view.

## Relation to Existing dbt

### Already modelled in this domain
All eight tables profiled are **existing marts** built on a complete `staging_nba` layer:

- **Staging (7):** `stg_nba_games`, `stg_nba_players`, `stg_nba_team_histories`, `stg_nba_player_statistics`, `stg_nba_player_statistics_extended`, `stg_nba_team_statistics`, `stg_nba_team_statistics_extended`
- **Marts (8):** `nba_game_results`, `nba_team_season_summary`, `nba_team_advanced_profile`, `nba_team_head_to_head`, `nba_team_dim`, `nba_player_career_stats`, `nba_player_season_stats`, `nba_player_advanced_career`

The domain is **mature** — traditional + advanced, player + team, game/season/career grains all covered. The gap is cross-grain *combined* marts (record × efficiency) and dimension-enriched serving views, not new base aggregations.

### Internal join keys (within NBA marts)
| Join key | Connects | Enables |
|---|---|---|
| `team_id` | `nba_team_dim` ← every team mart | Readable franchise names on game/season/H2H/profile rows |
| `(team_id, season_start_year)` | `nba_team_season_summary` ⋈ `nba_team_advanced_profile` | Record-vs-efficiency season view |
| `(person_id)` | `nba_player_career_stats` ⋈ `nba_player_advanced_career` | Counting totals beside efficiency, one row per player |
| `person_id, season_start_year, game_type` | `nba_player_season_stats` → roll up to career | Reconcile season rows against the career mart |
| `primary_team_id` | `nba_player_season_stats` → `nba_team_dim` | Player's main team that season, with label |

### Cross-domain joins
The other domains in the project — **Spotify** (`top_artists_by_period`, `top_tracks_by_period`, `recently_played`), **Crypto** (`ohlcv_1m/1h`), **Payments** (`payment_status`, `payments_by_minute`) and **StatsBomb** (`sb_match_summary`, `sb_player_stats`) — share **no natural join key** with NBA. They are independent subject areas in the same warehouse.

The one conceptual parallel worth noting: **StatsBomb** is the other sports domain and uses the same player/team/match structure (`sb_player_stats` is one-row-per-player like `nba_player_career_stats`; `sb_match_summary` mirrors `nba_game_results`). There's no shared key, but the **modelling patterns are reusable** — e.g. the same "career rollup + minimum-appearances floor" and "match pivoted home/away" templates apply to both.
